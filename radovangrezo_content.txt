If you have a product people love, but still 60% of the population never heard of you, call up some hamsters to knock your socks off. Yet another client who called up on me to help them create a media-neutral long-term creative concept for all their brand communication. It took only the first 4 airings on TV, to bring 1,000 new unique visitors to the website; +300% vs. usual averages. But sales, that’s what we care about, too, right? Revenue share for new visitors vs. returning ones went up to 23% (compared to 15% before the campaign). Since people loved the hamsters and the song, we scrambled to quickly create more content they were asking for, like a 3 minutes long video. 50,000 people watched it in the first week, already. But customer also joined in the creation. Making their own 10 hour video loop and other YouTube versions generating 100+k views in 10 days. Then, there were 350k+ views of TikTok videos in response to our #dedoleschallenge followed up the same on Instagram . To quote the client, three weeks into the campaign, “I’m worried we’ll sell out everything we’ve produced.” Agency: Somebody&Somebody Year: 2020 If it works, keep doing it. The hamsters were back only 3 months later in the second campaign for Dedoles. This time we set out to see whether an ad campaign can break the law of summer being a slow season in socks sales. The usual deal for a telco campaign. Unlimited this. Unlimited that. A set long-term visual concept. Restraints on production timing and costs. And a tough client. Agency: Saatchi&Saatchi/Publicis Austria Year: 2009 T-Mobile Czech Republic – Contagious Laughter X-mas 2009 campaign for T-Mobile Czech Republic. Nobody had a clue what would happen when we’d sent a comedian out to invade people’s families by either trying to jump in their car’s trunk or photobombing (a word that nobody used back then even) their photographs. We were worried there might be some violent reactions. Instead, actually the Czechs turned out to be very mellow and welcoming. One family almost drove off with our actor! Still, we got the footage we needed and made some of the first candid camera commercials of a new era.
